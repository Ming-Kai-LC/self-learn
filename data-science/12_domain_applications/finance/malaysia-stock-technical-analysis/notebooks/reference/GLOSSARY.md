ğŸ’¡ **Why it matters**: Different trading strategies work in bear vs bull markets. In bear markets, consider cash or short positions.

ğŸ’¡ **Why it matters**: If you want to sell immediately, you receive the bid price. Higher bid = better deal for sellers.

ğŸ“ **Example**: Bid price is RM 1.23. If you place a market order to sell, you'll get RM 1.23.

ğŸ“ **Example**: 2020-2021 post-COVID recovery was a bull market - most stocks surged.

### Bullish
ğŸ“– **Definition**: Positive outlook expecting prices to rise. Someone who is bullish thinks prices will go up.

ğŸ“ **Example**: "I'm bullish on this stock" = "I think this stock will go up."

ğŸ“ **Example**: Stock traded between RM 1.18-1.25 all day, closed at RM 1.23. Closing price = RM 1.23.

ğŸ“– **Definition**: Short-term price decline (typically 10-20%) in an overall uptrend.

ğŸ’¡ **Why it matters**: Corrections are NORMAL and healthy. Not the same as a *bear market*. Good buying opportunities.

ğŸ“ **Example**: Stock rises from RM 1.00 to RM 2.00, then falls to RM 1.60 (20% correction), then continues up.

ğŸ“– **Definition**: When one line (indicator or *moving average*) crosses above or below another line.

ğŸ’¡ **Why it matters**: Crossovers generate trading signals. *Golden Cross* and *MACD* crossovers are examples.

ğŸ“ **Example**: When 50-day MA crosses above 200-day MA = Golden Cross (bullish signal).

ğŸ“– **Definition**: Buying and selling stocks within the same trading day. All positions closed before market closes.

ğŸ“– **Definition**: Pattern where stock price consistently makes lower *highs* and lower *lows*.

ğŸ’¡ **Why it matters**: Don't fight the trend! In downtrends, avoid buying or consider *short selling*.

ğŸ“– **Definition**: The price level at which you decide to sell a stock (either for profit or loss).

ğŸ“– **Definition**: Evaluating a stock by examining company financials, management, industry, economy.
