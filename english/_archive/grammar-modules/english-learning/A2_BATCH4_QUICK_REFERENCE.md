## Key Grammar Rules to Emphasize

### Comparatives (Module 16)
- One syllable: add -er (tall → taller)
- Two syllables ending in -y: change y to i, add -er (happy → happier)
- Multi-syllable: use more (beautiful → more beautiful)
- Always use 'than' when comparing

### Superlatives (Module 17)
- One syllable: add -est (tall → tallest)
- Two syllables ending in -y: change y to i, add -est (happy → happiest)
- Multi-syllable: use most (beautiful → most beautiful)
- Always use 'the' before superlatives

### As...As (Module 18)
- Affirmative: as + adjective/adverb + as
- Negative: not as + adjective/adverb + as
- Shows equality (affirmative) or inequality (negative)

### Adverbs (Module 19)
- Regular: adjective + -ly (quick → quickly)
- Irregular: well, fast, hard (NOT goodly, fastly, hardly)
- Adjective describes noun; adverb describes verb

### Intensifiers (Module 20)
- Very/Really: intensify (neutral or positive)
- Too: excessive (negative implication)
- Enough: sufficiency (comes AFTER adjective/adverb)
- Very vs Too: "very hot" (neutral), "too hot" (can't handle it)
