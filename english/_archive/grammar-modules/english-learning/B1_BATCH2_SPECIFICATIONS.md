## Module 6: Second Conditional - Hypothetical Present/Future

**Full Title:** Second Conditional - Unreal Present and Future
**Subtitle:** If + Past Simple, Would + Base Verb
**Grammar Focus:** Hypothetical present and unlikely future situations
**Estimated Time:** 6-7 hours
**Exercise Count:** 95 (high - this is THE unreal present form)
**Difficulty:** ★★★★☆ (Challenging - requires understanding of "unreal" concept)

### Core Concept

Second conditional expresses **unreal or unlikely** situations in the present or future.

**Formation:** If + past simple, would + base verb

**Key Understanding:** The past tense does NOT mean past time - it signals unreality!

**Use Cases:**
1. **Hypothetical present:** "If I had a million dollars, I would travel the world." (I don't have it)
2. **Unlikely future:** "If I won the lottery, I would buy a house." (very unlikely)
3. **Advice:** "If I were you, I would study harder." (I'm not you - unreal)
4. **Polite requests:** "It would be great if you could help me." (softer than first conditional)

### Distinction from First Conditional

| First Conditional | Second Conditional |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Real possibility | Unreal/unlikely situation |
| If it rains, I **will** stay home. | If it rained every day, I **would** stay home. |
| If I have time, I'll help. | If I had time, I would help. (I don't have time) |
| Future reference | Present/future but unreal |

## Module 8: Mixed Conditionals - Combining Time Frames

**Full Title:** Mixed Conditionals - Past and Present Combined
**Subtitle:** If + Past Perfect, Would + Verb (and vice versa)
**Grammar Focus:** Conditionals mixing different time frames
**Estimated Time:** 5-6 hours
**Exercise Count:** 90
**Difficulty:** ★★★★★ (Very challenging - requires understanding both 2nd and 3rd)

### Core Concept

Mixed conditionals combine different time frames:
- **Type 1:** Past condition → Present result
- **Type 2:** Present condition → Past result

**Type 1 (More common):**
"If I had studied medicine, I would be a doctor now."
- Condition: past (I didn't study medicine)
- Result: present (I'm not a doctor now)

**Type 2 (Less common):**
"If I were more organized, I would have remembered your birthday."
- Condition: present (I'm not organized - general characteristic)
- Result: past (I didn't remember - specific past event)

## Module 9: Conditional Alternatives - Unless, Provided That, As Long As

**Full Title:** Conditional Alternatives - Unless, Provided That, As Long As
**Subtitle:** Different Ways to Express Conditions
**Grammar Focus:** Alternative conditional expressions
**Estimated Time:** 5-6 hours
**Exercise Count:** 85
**Difficulty:** ★★★☆☆ (Moderate - vocabulary-focused)

### Core Concept

Alternative ways to express conditional relationships:

- **Unless** = if...not (exception)
- **Provided that / Providing that** = if (prerequisite/requirement)
- **As long as** = if/only if (requirement/condition)
- **Only if** = exclusive condition
- **In case** = as a precaution (NOT the same as "if")

### Key Distinctions

**Unless vs If...not:**
- "Unless you study, you'll fail" = "If you don't study, you'll fail"
- Unless = exception to negative result
- Followed by positive, not negative

**In Case vs If:**
- "Take an umbrella in case it rains" (precaution - might or might not rain)
- "Take an umbrella if it rains" (you'll take it WHEN it rains)
- In case = preparation, not condition

## Module 10: Wish and If Only - Regrets and Wishes

**Full Title:** Wish and If Only - Expressing Regrets and Desires
**Subtitle:** I Wish + Past Simple/Past Perfect
**Grammar Focus:** Wish structures for present and past regrets
**Estimated Time:** 5-6 hours
**Exercise Count:** 90
**Difficulty:** ★★★★☆ (Challenging - requires tense understanding)

### Core Concept

"Wish" and "If only" express desires for different reality:

**Wish + Past Simple:** Regret about present (want different present)
- "I wish I had more time." (I don't have enough time NOW)
- "I wish I spoke Spanish." (I don't speak it NOW)

**Wish + Past Perfect:** Regret about past (want different past)
- "I wish I had studied harder." (I didn't study hard enough - can't change now)
- "I wish I had taken that job." (I didn't take it - too late now)

**Wish + Would:** Annoyance about present behavior (wanting change)
- "I wish you would stop smoking." (you smoke - I want you to stop)
- "I wish it would stop raining." (it's raining - I want it to stop)

**If Only:** Emphatic wish (stronger emotion)
- "If only I were taller!" (strong wish)
- "If only I had known!" (strong regret)

### Comparison with Conditionals

| Structure | Time | Reality |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Wish + past simple | Present | Unreal present (like 2nd conditional) |
| Wish + past perfect | Past | Impossible past (like 3rd conditional) |
| Second conditional | Present/Future | Hypothetical |
| Third conditional | Past | Impossible past |
