## MODULE 16: COMPARATIVE ADJECTIVES - Making Comparisons

### Module Overview

**Title:** Comparative Adjectives - Making Comparisons
**Subtitle:** Bigger, Better, More Interesting
**Focus Area:** Formation and use of comparative adjectives to compare two people, places, or things
**Estimated Time:** 5-6 hours
**Exercise Count:** 85 exercises

### Learning Objectives

1. Form comparative adjectives correctly using -er and more
2. Apply spelling rules for comparative forms (bigger, hotter, easier)
3. Use 'than' appropriately in comparative sentences
4. Distinguish between one-syllable and multi-syllable adjective rules
5. Use irregular comparatives (good → better, bad → worse)

### Topics Covered

- Comparative formation with one-syllable adjectives (tall → taller)
- Comparative formation with two-syllable adjectives ending in -y (happy → happier)
- Comparative formation with multi-syllable adjectives (more beautiful)
- Spelling rules: doubling consonants, changing y to i
- Using 'than' in comparative sentences
- Irregular comparatives: better, worse, farther/further
- Comparing people, places, and things
- Expressing preferences with comparatives

- Always using 'the' before superlatives
- Irregular superlatives: best, worst, farthest/furthest

- Talking about personal bests
- Expressing opinions with superlatives

## MODULE 20: DESCRIPTIVE LANGUAGE - Intensifiers (very, quite, too, enough)

### Module Overview

**Title:** Descriptive Language - Intensifiers
**Subtitle:** Very, Quite, Too, Enough - Adding Degree and Intensity
**Focus Area:** Using intensifiers to modify adjectives and adverbs, expressing degree and sufficiency

3. Use 'too' to express excessive degree (with negative meaning)

- Really + adjective/adverb (really interesting)
- Quite + adjective/adverb (quite nice, quite well)
- Too + adjective/adverb (too hot, too quickly) - excessive/negative

- Very vs Too - Critical difference
- Position of intensifiers in sentences

## Appendix: Common Mistakes to Address

### Module 16 - Comparatives
- ❌ more better (WRONG) → ✓ better
- ❌ more big (WRONG) → ✓ bigger
- ❌ beautifuller (WRONG) → ✓ more beautiful
- ❌ I am tall than you (WRONG) → ✓ I am taller than you

### Module 17 - Superlatives
- ❌ most best (WRONG) → ✓ best
- ❌ biggest (missing 'the') → ✓ the biggest
- ❌ the most big (WRONG) → ✓ the biggest
- ❌ the taller (comparing more than 2) → ✓ the tallest

### Module 18 - As...As
- ❌ as tall than (WRONG) → ✓ as tall as
- ❌ not so tall as (acceptable but) → ✓ not as tall as (preferred)
- ❌ as more expensive as (WRONG) → ✓ as expensive as

### Module 19 - Adverbs
- ❌ She runs quick (WRONG) → ✓ She runs quickly
- ❌ He plays good (WRONG) → ✓ He plays well
- ❌ I work hardly (WRONG) → ✓ I work hard

### Module 20 - Intensifiers
- ❌ very much big (WRONG) → ✓ very big
- ❌ enough hot (WRONG) → ✓ hot enough
- ❌ It's very expensive, I can't buy it (should use 'too')
- ❌ too good (when meaning 'very good')
