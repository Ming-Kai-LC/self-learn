## ğŸ“Š Learning Path by Level

### A1 (Beginner) - 40 notebooks, ~80 hours

**You can**:
- Understand basic phrases
- Introduce yourself
- Ask simple questions
- Handle basic exchanges

**Focus**: Foundation building, pronunciation, basic grammar through use

**Writing**: Simple sentences, basic emails, personal information
**Listening**: Slow, clear speech on familiar topics
**Reading**: Simple texts, signs, short messages
**Speaking**: Basic interactions, introductions, simple requests

**Timeline**: 3-4 months with 5 hours/week

---

### A2 (Elementary) - 48 notebooks, ~100 hours

**You can**:
- Handle routine exchanges
- Describe background and experiences
- Express immediate needs
- Understand familiar topics

**Focus**: Expanding vocabulary, simple past/future, everyday situations

**Writing**: Short emails, descriptions, simple stories
**Listening**: Clear standard speech on familiar matters
**Reading**: Simple articles, routine correspondence
**Speaking**: Routine conversations, simple explanations

**Timeline**: 4-5 months with 5 hours/week

---

### B1 (Intermediate) - 60 notebooks, ~120 hours

**You can**:
- Handle most travel situations
- Describe experiences and events
- Explain opinions with simple reasoning
- Understand main points of clear speech

**Focus**: Consolidating basics, workplace English, confidence building

**Writing**: Coherent emails (150-200 words), simple reports
**Listening**: 70-80% comprehension of standard speech
**Reading**: Straightforward texts on familiar subjects
**Speaking**: 15-20 minute conversations, meetings (participation)

**Timeline**: 5-7 months with 12 hours/week (from A2)

---

### B2 (Upper-Intermediate) - 80 notebooks, ~160 hours

**You can**:
- Interact fluently with native speakers
- Understand complex texts in your field
- Present clear, detailed arguments
- Produce detailed texts on various subjects

**Focus**: Professional fluency, advanced grammar, natural communication

**Writing**: Professional emails/reports (300-500 words), proposals
**Listening**: 80-90% comprehension, some content without subtitles
**Reading**: Complex texts including technical material
**Speaking**: Lead meetings, debates, complex negotiations

**Timeline**: 5-7 months with 12 hours/week (from B1)

**Key milestone**: This is the threshold for most professional environments!

---

### C1 (Advanced) - 60 notebooks, ~120 hours

**You can**:
- Express ideas fluently and spontaneously
- Use language flexibly for all purposes
- Understand virtually all spoken/written content
- Produce clear, well-structured detailed texts

**Focus**: Native-like fluency, idioms, cultural nuances, subtle meanings

**Writing**: Sophisticated texts with stylistic variety
**Listening**: 90%+ comprehension including idioms and humor
**Reading**: Long complex texts, appreciate style differences
**Speaking**: Fluent expression without searching for words

**Timeline**: 7-11 months with 12 hours/week (from B2)

**Key milestone**: Natives occasionally forget you're non-native!

---

### C2 (Proficiency) - 40 notebooks, ~80 hours

**You can**:
- Understand everything with ease
- Express yourself spontaneously and precisely
- Differentiate finer shades of meaning
- Produce clear, smoothly flowing texts

**Focus**: Perfection, specialized domains, near-native proficiency

**Timeline**: 6-12 months with 10 hours/week (from C1)

**Key milestone**: Functional native-level competence!

## ğŸ¯ Skill-Specific Strategies

### Writing
- Write daily (even 5 minutes)
- Use Grammarly/Khan Academy for every piece
- Read excellent writing in your field
- Keep a writing journal
- Practice different formats (email, report, essay)

### Listening
- Listen daily (commute, exercise, chores)
- Start with transcripts, then remove them
- Vary accents (UK, US, AU)
- Take notes while listening
- Discuss content with AI afterwards

### Reading
- Read 20 min daily
- Mix difficulty levels (80% comfortable, 20% challenging)
- Extract vocabulary in context
- Summarize to AI in your own words
- Read your professional field in English

### Speaking
- Speak English daily (even to AI)
- Record yourself regularly
- Shadow native speakers
- Practice pronunciation with ELSA
- Don't fear mistakesâ€”fluency > accuracy initially
