## Comprehensive Assessments (All Skills)

### 1. EF SET (‚≠ê PRIMARY FREE CERTIFICATION)

**Provider**: EF Education First
**Link**: https://www.efset.org/

#### Quick Overview
- **Cost**: 100% Free
- **Duration**: 15 min (quick) or 50 min (certificate)
- **Certificate**: Yes, shareable on LinkedIn/CV
- **Skills**: Reading + Listening
- **CEFR**: A1 to C2
- **Validity**: Widely recognized, not official exam replacement

#### Features
- ‚úÖ Completely free, no hidden costs
- ‚úÖ Professionally developed by language experts
- ‚úÖ Adaptive difficulty (adjusts to your level)
- ‚úÖ Instant results with detailed breakdown
- ‚úÖ Downloadable PDF certificate
- ‚úÖ LinkedIn integration
- ‚úÖ Can retake every 3 months

#### How to Use

**Initial Assessment** (Before starting program):
1. Create free account
2. Choose 50-minute comprehensive test
3. Find quiet space, use headphones
4. Complete reading section (25 min)
5. Complete listening section (25 min)
6. Receive CEFR level + score (0-100)
7. Record in `00_START_HERE/skill_assessment.ipynb`

**Progress Checks**:
- Month 3-4: First re-test
- Month 6-7: B1‚ÜíB2 verification
- Month 12: Major milestone
- Every 6 months: Track progress

**Score Interpretation**:
- 0-30: A1 (Beginner)
- 31-40: A2 (Elementary)
- 41-50: B1 (Intermediate)
- 51-60: B2 (Upper-Intermediate) ‚≠ê Professional threshold
- 61-70: C1 (Advanced)
- 71-100: C2 (Proficient)

#### Pro Tips
- Don't study specifically for EF SET‚Äîit measures real ability
- Take in morning when fresh
- Close all tabs/apps to avoid interruptions
- Use wired internet if possible
- Read instructions carefully for each section
- Don't rush‚Äîyou have enough time

**Limitations**:
- No speaking or writing assessment
- Not accepted for university admissions (use IELTS/TOEFL for that)
- Can only test every 3 months minimum

### 2. Duolingo English Test

**Provider**: Duolingo
**Link**: https://englishtest.duolingo.com/

#### Quick Overview
- **Cost**: $59 USD per attempt
- **Duration**: ~1 hour
- **Certificate**: Yes, official score report
- **Skills**: Reading, Writing, Listening, Speaking (all 4!)
- **CEFR**: 10-160 scale (approximately A2-C2)
- **Validity**: Accepted by 4,000+ universities worldwide

#### Features
- ‚úÖ Test all four skills (speaking included!)
- ‚úÖ Take from home, any time
- ‚úÖ Results in 48 hours
- ‚úÖ Adaptive difficulty
- ‚úÖ AI proctoring (no human proctor)
- ‚úÖ Widely accepted for university admissions
- ‚úÖ Much cheaper than TOEFL ($195) or IELTS ($250)

#### When to Use
- Need official score for university application
- Want comprehensive 4-skill assessment
- Prefer at-home testing
- Budget-conscious (vs IELTS/TOEFL)

#### Score Interpretation
- 10-55: Below A2 (Not recommended for admission)
- 60-85: A2-B1 (Basic user)
- 90-115: B1-B2 (Independent user)
- 120-140: B2-C1 (Proficient user)
- 145-160: C1-C2 (Advanced proficient)

**Most universities require**: 100-120+ for admission

### 3. IELTS Indicator

**Provider**: British Council/IDP/Cambridge
**Link**: https://www.ielts.org/for-test-takers/ielts-indicator

#### Quick Overview
- **Cost**: $49 USD
- **Duration**: 2 hours 45 minutes
- **Certificate**: Yes (practice test certificate)
- **Skills**: All 4 (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking)
- **CEFR**: Band 1-9 (approximately A1-C2)

- Real IELTS-style questions
- Results in 7 days
- Good preview before official IELTS

#### When to Use
- Preparing for official IELTS
- Want realistic practice test
- Need to know expected IELTS band
- 1-2 months before taking real IELTS

**Note**: This is NOT accepted for university admission or visa. For official scores, take IELTS Academic or General Training at a test center.

## Understanding Your Scores

### CEFR Levels Explained

**A1 (Beginner)**:
- Can introduce self
- Understand basic phrases
- Simple interactions
- **Estimated study time from zero**: 80-120 hours

**A2 (Elementary)**:
- Routine exchanges
- Describe background
- Immediate needs
- **Estimated study time from A1**: 100-150 hours

**B1 (Intermediate)**:
- Most travel situations
- Describe experiences
- Simple explanations
- **Estimated study time from A2**: 200-300 hours
- **Total from zero**: 380-570 hours

**B2 (Upper-Intermediate)**:
- Interact with natives fluently
- Complex texts in your field
- Detailed arguments
- **Estimated study time from B1**: 300-400 hours
- **Total from zero**: 680-970 hours
- ‚≠ê **Professional threshold**

**C1 (Advanced)**:
- Fluent and spontaneous
- Flexible language use
- Virtually all content
- **Estimated study time from B2**: 400-600 hours
- **Total from zero**: 1,080-1,570 hours

**C2 (Proficient)**:
- Near-native proficiency
- Understand everything
- Precise expression
- **Estimated study time from C1**: 400-600 hours
- **Total from zero**: 1,480-2,170 hours

## What to Do With Results

### If Results Show Progress ‚úÖ

1. **Celebrate**: You're improving!
2. **Analyze**: Which skill improved most? Why?
3. **Continue**: Current approach is working
4. **Adjust**: Can you replicate success in weaker skills?

### If Results Show No Progress ‚è∏Ô∏è

**Don't panic**. Plateaus are normal. Ask yourself:

1. **Consistency**: Did I practice 12+ hours/week?
2. **Active practice**: Or just passive consumption?
3. **AI interaction**: Daily speaking/writing with AI?
4. **Challenge level**: Am I working at edge of ability?
5. **Tracking**: Did I log errors and review them?

**Plateau breakers**:
- Increase study hours 25%
- Switch focus to weakest skill
- Increase difficulty level
- More AI conversation practice
- Add immersion (change phone to English)

### If One Skill Lags Behind üìâ

**Common scenario**: Listening/Reading at B2, but Speaking/Writing at B1

**Solution**:
- Dedicate 60% of time to lagging skill
- Daily practice mandatory for weak skill
- Reduce time on strong skills temporarily
- Track weak skill progress weekly

## AI-Powered Self-Assessment

### ChatGPT/Claude Assessment Prompts

**Speaking level check**:
```
I want to assess my English speaking level. Let's have a 10-minute conversation about [topic]. After we finish, please:

1. Estimate my CEFR level based on:
   - Grammar accuracy
   - Vocabulary range
   - Fluency and coherence
   - Pronunciation (if using voice)

2. Identify my top 3 strengths
3. Identify my top 3 areas for improvement
4. Suggest specific practice activities

Let's start: [begin conversation]
```

**Writing level check**:
```
I'm going to write a 300-word essay on [topic]. After I submit it, please:

1. Estimate my CEFR writing level
2. Analyze:
   - Task achievement
   - Coherence and cohesion
   - Vocabulary range
   - Grammatical accuracy
3. Provide specific improvement suggestions
4. Rewrite one paragraph at the next CEFR level to show me the target

Here's my essay: [paste essay]
```

**Vocabulary level check**:
```
Let's test my vocabulary. Please:

1. Ask me to define 20 words from B2 level
2. Ask me to use 10 C1 level words in sentences
3. Based on my responses, estimate my vocabulary level
4. Suggest specific vocabulary areas to focus on
```

## Red Flags: When Assessments Show Problems

### Warning Signs

‚ùå **No improvement after 3 months** (100+ hours study)
- Review study method quality
- Increase active practice (speaking/writing)
- Reduce passive consumption (just watching/reading)

‚ùå **One skill significantly behind** (2+ CEFR sublevels)
- Dedicate 60% time to that skill
- Daily practice mandatory
- Find specific resources for weak skill

‚ùå **Vocabulary not growing** (same size after 3 months)
- Not learning new words systematically
- Add WordUp daily practice
- Read more challenging material

‚ùå **Speaking stuck at B1 while reading at B2+**
- Not enough speaking practice
- Increase Gliglish to premium (unlimited)
- Find conversation partner
- Daily AI conversations non-negotiable
