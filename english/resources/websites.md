# Websites and Online Resources for English Learning

Comprehensive collection of free websites organized by purpose and CEFR level.

## Reading Practice Websites

### Breaking News English (â­ PRIMARY for B1-C1)
- **Level**: 7 difficulty levels (0-6)
- **Link**: https://breakingnewsenglish.com/
- **Features**: â­ Same article at 7 different levels, audio, exercises, vocabulary
- **Updates**: 2 new articles weekly
- **Topics**: Current events, world news
- **Best for**: Reading at your exact level, vocabulary building
- **How to use**:
  1. Choose article at your level
  2. Read without audio first
  3. Listen while reading
  4. Complete comprehension exercises
  5. Review vocabulary list
  6. Discuss article with AI

### News in Levels
- **Level**: 3 levels (Level 1: A2, Level 2: B1, Level 3: B2+)
- **Link**: https://www.newsinlevels.com/
- **Features**: Daily news, same story at 3 levels, audio included
- **Updates**: Daily
- **Topics**: International news, simple language
- **Best for**: Daily reading habit, news vocabulary

### British Council Learn English
- **Level**: A1-C1 (organized by level)
- **Link**: https://learnenglish.britishcouncil.org/
- **Features**: Reading passages, listening exercises, grammar, vocabulary
- **Updates**: Regular
- **Topics**: Diverse (work, travel, culture, technology)
- **Best for**: Structured learning, British English

### VOA Learning English

## Daily Practice Schedule by Level

### A2-B1: Building Foundation

**Morning (20 min)**:
- News in Levels (Level 1-2): Read + listen to one article
- Write 5 new vocabulary words in notebook

**Lunch (15 min)**:
- Randall's ESL Lab: One easy/medium listening activity
- Complete quiz

**Evening (30 min)**:
- British Council Learn English: One reading + one grammar exercise
- Practice new grammar with AI

### B1-B2: Skill Development

**Morning (20 min)**:
- Breaking News English (Level 3-4): Read article
- Complete exercises

**Lunch (15 min)**:
- TED Talk: Watch one short talk (6-10 min)
- Summarize main points

**Evening (40 min)**:
- Write & Improve: Write on given topic (20 min)
- Review AI feedback and revise (20 min)

### B2-C1: Advanced Practice

**Morning (20 min)**:
- BBC News or The Guardian: Read one article
- Extract 5 advanced expressions

**Lunch (15 min)**:
- NPR or BBC: Listen to news podcast
- Note key points

**Evening (50 min)**:
- Breaking News English (Level 5-6) or The Economist: Deep reading
- Discuss article with AI in depth
- Practice using new vocabulary in sentences

## Weekly Challenge Ideas

### Week 1: Reading Challenge
- Read one Breaking News English article daily at increasing difficulty
- Track comprehension percentage
- Goal: 80%+ comprehension without dictionary

### Week 2: Listening Challenge
- Listen to one Randall's ESL activity daily
- Start "difficult" level
- Goal: 70%+ quiz score without transcript

### Week 3: Writing Challenge
- Write 300 words daily on Write & Improve
- Various formats: email, essay, report, letter
- Goal: Maintain or improve CEFR score

### Week 4: Vocabulary Challenge
- Learn 50 new words via Vocabulary.com
- Use each in sentence with AI
- Goal: Active recall at end of week

## Integration with AI Practice

After using any website:

### For Reading (Breaking News, News in Levels)
```
I just read an article about [topic] on [website]. Here's my summary: [summary]

Please:
1. Check my summary for accuracy and grammar
2. Explain 3 key vocabulary words I found challenging
3. Ask me 5 comprehension questions about the article
4. Let's discuss: What do you think about [topic]?
```

### For Listening (Randall's, ELLLO)
```
I listened to a conversation about [topic]. I understood:
- Main idea: [X]
- Details: [Y]
- Vocabulary: [Z]

Please verify my understanding and quiz me on what I might have missed.
```

### For Writing (Write & Improve)
```
I wrote a [type] on Write & Improve and received [CEFR level] score.
The feedback mentioned: [key points]

Can you help me improve this paragraph: [paste paragraph]
Focus on: [vocabulary/grammar/clarity]
```
